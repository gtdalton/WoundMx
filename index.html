Wound Management

X-rays

All glass injuries require routine x-ray to screen for a foreign body

Local Anaesthetic

  1. Clean the skin with an alcowipe
  2. Use an orange needle to inject lignocaine
  3. Start at the proximal end of the wound to provide quickest analgaesia.
Tip

Use a good volume of anaesthetic and go in a wide enough area around the wound to cover where your sutures will go

Cleaning

  1. Thoroughly irrigate inside the wound and clean the skin in a generous area around the wound.
  2. If appropriate, this can be done by the patient themselves sitting on a chair pulled up to the sink with tap water (no need for sterile water).
  3. If the wound is too deep to be sure that is is adequately washed out, consider senior/specialist review for need for exploration under anaesthesia.
  4. Finally, sterilise the area using a swab to apply chlorhexidine to the wound and surrounding skin

Suturing

Location Removal time (Days)
Face3-5*
Scalp7

High tension areas
(joints, hands, back) 		10-14*
*Removing facial sutures early prevents "rail-road" scarring developing, while high tension sutures should be left in longer to prevent the wound re-opening after removal
